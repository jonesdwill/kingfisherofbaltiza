🧭 About

Meet Meg Loftus and the steel-hulled Kingfisher of Baltiza — the skipper and her vessel.

👩‍✈️

Meg Loftus

Meg has been sailing since she was a teenager, first racing dinghies on Scottish sea lochs, then crewing on offshore passages to Ireland, Norway, and beyond. She gained her Yachtmaster Offshore certificate in her twenties and has since accumulated thousands of sea miles as skipper.

The idea of sailing to Greenland took root during a winter spent reading everything she could find about high-latitude voyaging. After years of careful saving, planning, and searching for the right boat, she found Kingfisher of Baltiza — a sturdy steel sloop built for exactly this kind of challenge.

Meg works as a marine biologist when not at sea, which makes Greenland's extraordinary wildlife an additional, very personal draw to the expedition.

Kingfisher of Baltiza

Built in 1982 by a small yard in the Netherlands, Kingfisher of Baltiza is a 38-foot steel sloop of the Van de Stadt design — a type well regarded for offshore cruising. Her 6mm steel hull is ideal for high-latitude sailing where the occasional brush with ice is more than a theoretical risk.

Meg bought her in 2021 and has spent four years upgrading her for Arctic work: new standing rigging, an upgraded windvane self-steering system, a Webasto diesel heating system, an ice pole, reinforced bow fendering, and a full suite of modern electronic navigation equipment backed up with a complete set of paper charts.
