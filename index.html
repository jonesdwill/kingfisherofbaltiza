⛵ Kingfisher of Baltiza

Meet the Boat

Kingfisher of Baltiza is a robust offshore cruising yacht, built for the demands of high-latitude sailing. With her deep keel, reinforced hull, and sea-kindly lines, she's more than ready for the challenging waters of the North Atlantic and the icebound coasts of Greenland.

Skipper Meg Loftus has spent years preparing boat and crew for this adventure — from rigging upgrades to ice-navigation training and cold-water safety drills. This website chronicles the journey from planning all the way to landfall in Greenland and the return home.

The Journey

Expedition Highlights

From Scotland's west coast to the fiords of Greenland — every leg of the route has its own challenges and rewards.

🏴󠁧󠁢󠁳󠁣󠁴󠁿

Departure — Scotland

Setting sail from the west coast of Scotland, threading through the Hebrides before turning north-west into open ocean.

🇮🇸

Waypoint — Iceland

A resupply and rest stop in Reykjavík before tackling the final and most remote leg to the Greenlandic coast.

🧊

Greenland — East Coast

Navigating among icebergs and through dramatic fiords to reach some of the most breathtaking and remote anchorages on Earth.

🌅

Return Passage

The homeward route via the Faroe Islands and back to Scotland, completing a round trip of roughly 3,400 nautical miles.
