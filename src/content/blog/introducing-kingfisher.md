Welcome to the website of **Kingfisher of Baltiza**, and her skipper, Meg Loftus.

Kingfisher is a 32-foot William Atkin *Eric* class yacht, built in Malaysia in 1974 from Chengai — a dense, bombproof tropical hardwood well suited to the kind of sailing she was designed for. She carries a Bermudan ketch rig on a keel-stepped main mast and a deck-stepped mizzen, a setup that makes her manageable offshore and gives her a distinctive, workmanlike profile.

She is not a modern boat, and she was never meant to be. The *Eric* design is a wholesome, seaworthy double-ender with a reputation for going to sea and coming back again. Her most famous sister ship is **Suhali**, the vessel Sir Robin Knox-Johnston sailed to complete the first-ever solo, non-stop circumnavigation of the globe in the 1968–1969 Sunday Times Golden Globe Race. That lineage is not lost on us.

Kingfisher underwent a thorough refit in 2015. Shortly afterwards Meg acquired her and continued to work on her at the **Johnson and Loftus boatyard in Ullapool**, on the northwest coast of Scotland — a fitting base for a boat intended for cold, high-latitude sailing.

Since then she has been prepared, improved, and pushed gradually further towards the kind of sailing she was built for. This summer, that means **Greenland**.

In July 2026 Kingfisher will make for Skjoldungen Fjord in Southeast Greenland — a remote and rarely visited stretch of coastline accessible only by sea. This site will document the preparation, the passage, and whatever comes after.
